About J.K. Rowling

Joanne Rowling, AKA J.K. Rowling, was born on 31st July 1965. She is a British Author. She is well known for her famous series, Harry Potter. She is also a philanthropist, a film producer, a television producer and a screenwriter. She and her husband, Neil Murray, currently have 3 children: Jessica Isabel Rowling Arantes, David Gordon Rowling Murray and Mackenzie Jean Rowling Murray.

Facts about Joanne Rowling

  • She was rejected by 12 publishers since they thought the 1st book was "too long"! Her 13th publisher had given the book's 1st chapter to his daughter who wanted more! The publisher published the book which became a huge success.

  • The time when Rowling's 1st book was almost published, people asked her to change her name. They beleived that boys wouldn't like to read a book written by a woman. Joanne thus and a K was added to her name which stands for Kathleen, her maternal grandmother, who was also her godmother! Thus, getting her the pen-name, J.K. Rowling.

  • Joanne was also forced to change the name of her 1st book, 'The Philosopher's Stone' to 'Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone' since they thought that kids would want to read a book with the title 'Sorcerer' rather than a book with the title 'Philosopher' since the name didn't sound 'magical' to describe the story. Rowling regreted this afterword but since she was under difficulties that time, she had agreed to change the name of her book.
